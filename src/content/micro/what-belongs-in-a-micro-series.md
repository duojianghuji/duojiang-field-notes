Micro photographs often become more interesting when they are grouped by a clear visual rule.

One useful rule is material: bark, stone, leaf surfaces, paper fibers, rust, glass, or insect structures. Another is gesture: repeating curves, broken edges, clustered dots, or directional grain. The point is not to over-explain the image, but to give the series a backbone.

This section can hold more than galleries. It can also include short notes on scale, lighting, magnification, or the moment when an ordinary object stops reading as ordinary. Those notes help the photographs feel like observations instead of loose files.

Over time, each series can become a small archive of how your eye changes.
